POV 2: Jessica - AI & Environmental Concerns

Jessica is hesitant about AI due to its negative environmental impacts. AI is a powerful tool, but she sees its cost as too great to justify using it.

Amp up the good:

  • HMW highlight AI’s potential for solving environmental issues to make Jessica see it as a tool for sustainability?
  • HMW showcase AI-powered sustainability projects to shift Jessica’s perspective?

Remove the bad:

  • HMW reduce AI’s carbon footprint so that Jessica and others can use it without guilt?
  • HMW create AI tools that prioritize energy efficiency?

Explore the opposite:

  • HMW make AI a net-positive force for the environment instead of a burden?
  • HMW make AI’s environmental impact as visible as its benefits to increase accountability?

Question an assumption:

  • HMW develop AI models that don’t require massive computational power?
  • HMW make AI’s sustainability transparent to end users?

Go after adjectives:

  • HMW turn “resource-heavy AI” into “eco-friendly AI”?
  • HMW design AI that is perceived as “clean” rather than wasteful?

ID unexpected resources:

  • HMW leverage renewable energy sources for AI training and usage?
  • HMW partner with environmental organizations to create green AI policies?

Create an analogy:

  • HMW make AI training as energy-efficient as a Tesla compared to a gas car?
  • HMW treat AI like an ecosystem, where resources are recycled instead of wasted?

Change a status quo:

  • HMW push for industry standards that require AI to be sustainable?
  • HMW make AI users accountable for their environmental impact?

Break POV into pieces:

  • HMW educate users about AI’s environmental impact?
  • HMW incentivize companies to build AI with sustainability in mind?

  • HMW help AI distinguish between fact and opinion?

Explore the opposite:

  • HMW make AI more trustworthy than Google for historical research?
  • HMW make AI-powered research feel as rigorous as a university library search?

Question an assumption:

  • HMW train AI on verifiable, peer-reviewed sources only?
  • HMW give AI a "credibility score" for each response?

Go after adjectives:

  • HMW make AI-generated information “verifiable” instead of “questionable”?
  • HMW make AI answers feel “rigorous” rather than “shallow”?

ID unexpected resources:

  • HMW use blockchain to track AI’s sources for transparency?
  • HMW let AI cross-reference multiple reputable sources before generating an answer?

Create an analogy:

  • HMW make AI a “historian’s research assistant” rather than a guesswork generator?
  • HMW turn AI into an academic librarian rather than a casual chatbot?

Change a status quo:

  • HMW force AI to cite sources in the same way research papers do?
  • HMW hold AI developers accountable for misinformation?

Break POV into pieces:

  • HMW make AI’s responses auditable by experts?
  • HMW ensure AI doesn’t “hallucinate” incorrect facts?
