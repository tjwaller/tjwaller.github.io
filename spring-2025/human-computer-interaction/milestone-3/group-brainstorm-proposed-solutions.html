Group Brainstorm - Proposed Solutions

These are brainstormed solutions to the Best How Might We Questions

1. HMW make AI a net-positive force for the environment instead of a burden?

Run AI datacenters off of Solar and Nuclear Energy only. Maybe do this with Policy changes.

using AI to calculate how to best restore ecosystems based on certain parameters (remembering AI isn't just LLMs)

Bio-inspired computing: How can we mimic the efficiency we find in nature?

Use Satalite images from NOAA and machine learning to predict and monitor rainforest and other ecological changes that we may need to be notified about.

Use these same images to generate models of the collapsing ecosystems to better help reverse it.

LaJohn's submissions:
Repurpose the output of AI in ways that would allow us to reduce our environmental footprint elsewhere.

  • I.e Repurpose generated heat to be used in heating buildings.

Dedicate more time and resources toward powering AI systems dedicated to helping the
environment

  • Essentially using AI to alleviate the issues of AI

Invest in creating more versatile cooling systems

  • I.e create a more optimized cooling fluid that drives away the need for large
    bodies of water.

8/15

2. HMW use idle computational power from personal devices to decentralize AI training?

I love this one. There's something like this called SETI. We can use this approach to create a FOSS AI model that can then be run locally on services like OLLAMA

Have a screensaver people can run on their computer. When they aren't running the computer the screensaver turns on showing them that their IDLE computer is training AI models from around the globe.

Create a big initiative to get people to want to be a part of something bigger and use their machines to train an AI model that will do a lot of good for the world.

Maybe use docker to allow people to easily deploy on their own machines

Figure out how to allocate recourses to different machines based on their download/upload rate as well as their idle processing power (without going overboard and overheating their CPUs)

LaJohn's Submissions:
Provide users with an opt-in system that allows users to have their personal devices used for AI training in exchange for benefits (I.e product discounts).

Gamify AI training in a way that could make it fun for the user to interact with

6/15

3. HMW change the way AI models are trained or run to make them less resource-intensive?

create a weaker AI with a larger context window to allow it to reason better

Create custom AI chips that produce less heat and use less energy

LaJohn's submissions:
Minimize resource waste by focusing on reusability

  • Reuse water for cooling
  • Build databases off of recycled minerals and materials.

Give LLM users the option to lower the haste/quality of responses so that AI queries can be less computationally intensive (and thus use less energy, generate less heat, etc).

Optimize data-center infrastructure and systems (especially GPUs) to either better accommodate high temperatures or generate less heat overall.

5/15
